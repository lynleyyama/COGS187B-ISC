Member Organizations

BECOME A MEMBER ORGANIZATION

1. Designate a member from your organization to represent your organization at ISC meetings and GBMs, they will be referred as the ISC Delegate

2. Designate another member from your organization to attend event planning meetings, they will be referred as the ISC Committee Member.

The Inter-Sustainbility Council oversees the following organizations...

Environmental Justice Affairs

The ultimate purpose of the Office of Environmental Justice Affairs is to ensure that our Associated Students plays a significant role in the advocacy, outreach, policy and education of environmental justice within our UCSD community.

Association of Environmental Professionals

The Association of Environmental Professionals (AEP) is a non-profit organization established to facilitate greater relationships between professionals and foster mutual professional growth of our members through those relationships.

Aquaholics Anonymous

The mission of AQUAholics Anonymous is to encourage water conservation through education and outreach and to be a resource for networking and collaboration between different areas of campus that are working on water conservation.

CALPIRG

CALPIRG is a statewide, student-directed non-profit organization that works to solve problems facing our society, specifically by defending the public interest against powerful special interests.

Ecology, Behavior, and Evolution Club

The Ecology, Behavior & Evolution Club at UCSD seeks to provide students with research opportunities and information regarding careers in ecology, behavior & evolution (EBE).

Econauts

Through education, events, and programming, the Housing • Dining • Hospitality EcoNauts represent the department’s sustainable efforts and ensure we practice what we preach—recycle, conserve, and sustain!

Engineers for a Sustainable World

Engineers for a Sustainable World at UC San Diego is a group of passionate students striving to make a difference in the world through sustainable projects.

Food Recovery Network

Food Recovery Network at UCSD serves to provide a link between these parallel problems by diverting unwanted food "waste" to those in need.

Grow UCSD

Grow UCSD provides a forum for the collaboration of student gardens on campus. This includes opportunities for students to get involved with existing gardens, as well as educational, social, and research opportunities.

Muir Environmental Corps

Muir Environmental Corps (MEC) participates in environmental activities at the college and in the surrounding community. Outings include clean-up projects at the cliffs, and along streams and beach bluffs, supporting recycling efforts around the campus, and coordinating Muir’s Annual Whale Watching Trip.

Roger's Community Garden

Roger’s Community Garden at UCSD (formerly the Neighborhood Community Garden) is a student-led garden serving the UCSD and San Diego community aimed at offering land to students, staff, faculty, and alumni, to grow herbs, flowers, fruits and vegetables.

Sierra Club at UCSD

A group of UCSD students who want to represent the Sierra Club here at UCSD through environmental activism, ecological education, and outdoor recreation. They will be planning hikes and camping trips, as well as creating conservation projects on and off campus.

Staff Sustainability Network

The Staff Sustainability Network is focused on bringing awareness, transparency, and collaboration to existing sustainability efforts, providing support in pursuing new opportunities for positive impact, connecting faculty, staff, students, alumni, and community members with similar eco-passions.

Surfrider Foundation

Surfrider Foundatioin UCSD club works to keep our local beaches and ocean clean, while also keeping our marine wildlife safe!

Swipe Out Hunger

Through Swipes UC San Diego students are able to directly donate their dining dollars to alleviate hunger in San Diego and on campus.

Thurgood Marshall College Community Garden

As guided by the philosophy of Thurgood Marshall College, to develop the scholar and the citizen, the Community Garden will serve as an opportunity to learn about social responsibility through sustainable living, an outdoor study area and a means to develop a close-knit Marshall community.

Triton Food Pantry

The mission of the Triton Food Pantry is to provide a discreet service to UCSD students in need of food. We aim to build a network of food resources and awareness about food insecurity so that every UCSD student has nutritious fuel to achieve academic success.

Student Sustainability Collective

The Student Sustainability Collective (SSC) is a passionate group of student leaders dedicated to promoting sustainable policies, education for students, and social justice dialogue on our campus.

Sustainability Ambassadors

The Sustainability Ambassadors are a registered campus student organization based on the Carbon Neutrality Initiative. This program aims to educate and engage UC San Diego students through peer education and programming to raise awareness of UC's goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2025.

University Centers

Price Center East expansion received a LEED silver equivalent rating for sustainable elements and construction practices. The new Sustainability Resource Center is housed in Price Center West, creating visibility for sustainable efforts and enhancing collaboration among students, staff and faculty.
